Featured Publications

Explore these publications to see geospatial impact evaluation in action across diverse contexts and methodologies:

Working Paper #44
A Primer on Geospatial Impact Evaluation Methods, Tools, and Applications
Ariel BenYishay, Daniel Runfola, Rachel Trichler, et al. • 2017

The foundational GIE methodology paper introducing how georeferenced data on development investments can be fused with remotely sensed outcomes to conduct quasi-experimental evaluations at substantially lower cost than traditional RCTs.

Journal Article
Geospatial Impact Evaluation of a Low-Cost Agricultural Intervention for Enhancing Environmental Resilience
Khattri et al. • International Journal of Applied Earth Observation • 2025

Evaluates sugarcane cultivation as an environmental intervention in western Nepal using remote sensing data, optimal full matching, and difference-in-differences to assess impacts on soil erosion and riverbank stabilization.

Assessing the Spatiotemporal Dynamics of Seasonal and Perennial Surface Water Resources Across Lesotho's Agroecological Zones
Kunwar Singh, Sara Sayedi, Ariel BenYishay, Tšepiso A. Rantšo • 2025

Analyzes surface water availability across Lesotho's diverse agroecological zones using satellite imagery and geospatial methods to inform agricultural planning and climate adaptation strategies.

Spatiotemporal Prediction of Conflict Fatality Risk Using Convolutional Neural Networks and Satellite Imagery
Seth Goodman, Ariel BenYishay, Daniel Runfola • Remote Sensing • 2024

Demonstrates how convolutional neural networks analyzing satellite imagery can predict conflict fatality risk, showcasing machine learning applications in geospatial impact evaluation.

Working Paper
Reducing the Costs and Barriers to Evaluations Using Geospatial Data: New Methods with an Application for HIV/AIDS in Côte d'Ivoire
AidData Working Paper • 2020

Introduces methods using geospatially interpolated data to reduce evaluation costs, demonstrating how predicted surface data combined with survey data can detect small treatment effects in health interventions.
