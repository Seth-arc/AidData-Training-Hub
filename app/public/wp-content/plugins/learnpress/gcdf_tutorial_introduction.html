Tutorial Series Introduction

Global Chinese Development Finance
Dataset Tutorial

Welcome to AidData's Global Chinese Development Finance Dataset (GCDF) tutorial series. This comprehensive learning experience will guide you through one of the world's most extensive datasets tracking China's overseas development program—a uniquely granular resource that captures grants and loans worth $2.2 trillion across more than 200 countries from 2000 to 2023.

At AidData, we pioneered the Tracking Underreported Financial Flows (TUFF) methodology to systematically document China's overseas lending and grant-giving activities—financial flows that remain largely unreported by traditional international finance tracking systems. Since China became a financier of first resort for many low- and middle-income countries in the early 21st century, understanding the scale, scope, and terms of these financial commitments has become essential for evidence-based policymaking, academic research, and informed public discourse.

Why the Global Chinese Development Finance Dataset?
Unprecedented Scale and Comprehensiveness: The GCDF dataset tracks 30,000+ projects financed by 1,193 Chinese official sector donors and lenders across 217 countries—capturing a development finance portfolio vastly larger than previously understood.
Filling Critical Evidence Gaps: Because China's state-backed lenders and donors rarely disclose project-level data or country-by-country breakdowns, this dataset addresses a major knowledge gap about the socio-economic, environmental, and geopolitical implications of Chinese development finance.
Global Research Impact: Earlier versions of our GCDF dataset have led to more than 250 publications cited over 4,000 times in academic literature and 450 times by leading journalists, shaping policy debates worldwide.

What You'll Learn

This tutorial series will guide you through the structure, methodology, and applications of the GCDF dataset. You will learn to:

Navigate the dataset structure and understand key variables for tracking Chinese development finance
Understand the TUFF methodology used to systematically track underreported financial flows
Distinguish between different types of Chinese official finance, including grants, loans, and pledges
Query and analyze the dataset using China.AidData.org and other research tools
Apply the data to research questions about debt sustainability, infrastructure development, and geopolitical influence
About This Series

Each module in this tutorial series includes video instruction, detailed explanations of dataset variables and methodology, and practical examples drawn from AidData's research. The modules are designed to be completed sequentially, building your understanding of both the data and its applications. Whether you're a researcher, policy analyst, journalist, or development practitioner, this series will equip you with the knowledge to leverage one of the world's most comprehensive datasets on international development finance.

By the end of this tutorial series, you will be able to confidently navigate the GCDF dataset, understand the nuances of Chinese development finance, and conduct your own analyses to inform policy decisions, academic research, or public discourse.
