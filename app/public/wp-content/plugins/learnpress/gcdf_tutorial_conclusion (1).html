to Your Research

Congratulations on completing AidData's Global Chinese Development Finance Dataset tutorial series! The GCDF dataset has enabled groundbreaking research on China's overseas development program, informing policy decisions from Washington to Jakarta, shaping academic discourse, and filling critical evidence gaps about the world's largest bilateral creditor. The publications featured below showcase diverse applications—from debt sustainability analysis and infrastructure impact assessments to studies of geopolitical influence and environmental implications.

Featured Publications

Explore these flagship reports and studies that leverage the GCDF dataset to answer critical questions about Chinese development finance:

Chasing China: Learning to Play by Beijing's Global Lending Rules
Bradley C. Parks, Sheng Zhang, Brooke Escobar, et al. • Policy Report • 2025

Comprehensive analysis of China's $2.2 trillion overseas lending portfolio across 217 countries, revealing how Beijing has shifted focus toward sensitive industries and strategic lending while its financial operations become increasingly opaque.

How China Collateralizes
Anna Gelpern, Omar Haddad, Sebastian Horn, et al. • Working Paper • 2025

First comprehensive analysis of secured lending practices by Chinese creditors, presenting new data on $420 billion in collateralized public debt across 57 countries and revealing how cash collateral arrangements secure priority repayment.

Power Playbook: Beijing's Bid to Secure Overseas Transition Minerals
Brooke Escobar, Ammar A. Malik, Sheng Zhang, et al. • Policy Report • 2025

Tracks $56.9 billion in Chinese official financing for critical mineral operations across 19 countries, decoding Beijing's strategy for securing control over key segments of the global supply chain for copper, cobalt, lithium, and rare earths.

Investing in Narratives: How Beijing Promotes Its Development Projects in the Philippines
Bryan Burgess, Timothy Joseph G. Henares, et al. • Policy Report • 2024

Uncovers how China shapes media narratives around its infrastructure investments in the Philippines through state-run media, content-sharing partnerships, and journalist training, demonstrating the interplay between development finance and public diplomacy.

Into the Breach: Will China Step Up as the U.S. Retreats on Global Development?
Samantha Custer, Bryan Burgess, Narayani Sritharan • Policy Brief • 2025

Analyzes whether China will expand its development portfolio to fill gaps left by U.S. retrenchment, examining Beijing's evolving approach to overseas lending and its implications for global development cooperation.

Methodology Paper
Tracking Loans and Grants from China to Low-, Middle-, and High-Income Countries: An Application of AidData's TUFF 4.0 Methodology
Bradley C. Parks, Brooke Escobar, Katherine Walsh, et al. • Methodology Paper • 2025

Detailed guide to the 4.0 version of AidData's Tracking Underreported Financial Flows (TUFF) methodology, codifying systematic procedures for collecting information about aid and credit from official sector donors who don't publish comprehensive data.
