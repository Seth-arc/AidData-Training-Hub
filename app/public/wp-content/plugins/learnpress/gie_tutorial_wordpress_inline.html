Module 01 · 7–8 minutes

Introduction to Geospatial
Impact Evaluation

Discover what makes geospatial impact evaluation a powerful tool for development research. Learn how GIE fills the 'missing middle' between randomized controlled trials and performance evaluations, and understand when GIE is the appropriate methodology for your evaluation needs.

Learning Objectives
Understanding GIE: Recognize what geospatial impact evaluation is and how it differs from traditional evaluation approaches.
Advantages Over Traditional Methods: Learn how GIE can establish a counterfactual retroactively using geographic data, reducing cost and time compared to RCTs.
When to Use GIE: Identify development interventions that are suitable for geospatial impact evaluation, particularly those with geographic targeting.
02
Module 02 · 8–9 minutes

Understanding Spatial
Data Sources

Navigate the world of spatial data sources essential for geospatial impact evaluation. From satellite imagery platforms like Landsat and Sentinel to Google Earth Engine, learn how to access, combine, and work with both remote sensing and ground-based data for robust impact evaluation.

Learning Objectives
Key Spatial Data Types: Identify essential spatial data sources including Landsat, Sentinel-2, MODIS, and understand their respective strengths and applications.
Coordinate Systems: Understand coordinate reference systems, projections, and how to properly align spatial datasets to avoid analysis errors.
Working with Satellite Imagery: Access and process satellite imagery using platforms like Google Earth Engine for planetary-scale geospatial analysis.
Combining Data Sources: Learn to integrate remote sensing data with ground-based surveys while exercising caution with displaced coordinates in DHS data.
03
Module 03 · 9–10 minutes

Spatial Analysis
Techniques

Master the analytical methods that make geospatial impact evaluation rigorous and credible. Explore difference-in-differences with spatial data, matching techniques for GIE, regression discontinuity in geographic contexts, and learn to address spatial spillovers that can bias your results.

Learning Objectives
Difference-in-Differences: Apply difference-in-differences with spatial data, understanding how the interaction term estimates the treatment effect purged of common time trends.
Matching Techniques: Use matching to select control areas similar to treated areas on observable characteristics, improving the validity of your comparison groups.
Regression Discontinuity: Recognize when regression discontinuity design is appropriate for GIE—when there's a geographic cutoff where treatment assignment changes sharply.
Addressing Spillovers: Understand spatial spillover effects—when an intervention affects areas beyond the directly treated zone—and learn strategies to account for them.
04
Module 04 · 8–9 minutes

Case Study: Evaluating Agricultural
Infrastructure Impact

See geospatial impact evaluation in action through a real-world case study of irrigation infrastructure in Mali. Learn how NDVI (Normalized Difference Vegetation Index) was used to measure agricultural outcomes, how buffer zones addressed spillovers, and what the findings revealed about climate adaptation during drought years.

Learning Objectives
Real-World Application: Apply GIE concepts to evaluate the impact of irrigation infrastructure on agricultural productivity in Mali using satellite-derived vegetation indices.
Satellite-Based Outcomes: Interpret NDVI as a measure of vegetation health and photosynthetic activity, understanding why irrigated areas maintained higher NDVI during drought years.
Practical Challenges: Understand how buffer zones were created to exclude areas affected by spillovers from the control group, and other practical solutions to common GIE challenges.
05
Module 05 · 6–7 minutes

Best Practices and
Next Steps

Ensure the quality and credibility of your geospatial impact evaluation by learning essential best practices. Discover common pitfalls to avoid, quality assurance practices to implement, and resources for continued learning as you apply GIE methods to your own development research.

Learning Objectives
Common Pitfalls: Identify and avoid frequent errors in GIE such as misaligning coordinate reference systems between datasets, specification searching, and inadequate robustness checks.
Quality Assurance: Apply best practices including pre-analysis plans to commit to specific methods before examining outcomes, and validating satellite measures against ground truth data.
Resources for Continued Learning: Access the full AidData GIE Toolkit and additional resources to deepen your understanding and apply these methods to your own research projects.
