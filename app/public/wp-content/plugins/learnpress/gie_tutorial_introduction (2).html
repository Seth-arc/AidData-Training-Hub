Geospatial Impact Evaluation
Tutorial Series

Welcome to AidData's Geospatial Impact Evaluation (GIE) tutorial series. This comprehensive learning experience is designed to equip researchers, development practitioners, and policy analysts with the knowledge and skills to conduct rigorous impact evaluations using geospatial data and methods.

At AidData, we have pioneered the use of geospatial technologies to evaluate the impact of development interventions across the globe. Our work combines satellite imagery, geographic information systems (GIS), and econometric methods to answer critical questions about what works in international development—and for whom. By leveraging the power of geospatial data, we can evaluate projects at unprecedented scale and granularity, reaching places and populations that traditional evaluation methods often miss.

Why Geospatial Impact Evaluation?
Overcoming Traditional Constraints: Geospatial methods allow us to establish counterfactuals retroactively, reducing the cost and time constraints that often make randomized controlled trials infeasible for large-scale infrastructure and development projects.
Filling the Evidence Gap: GIE fills the "missing middle" between performance monitoring and rigorous impact evaluation, providing credible evidence where traditional experimental designs are impractical or impossible.
Real-World Applications: AidData has applied GIE methods to evaluate diverse development interventions—from transportation infrastructure and agricultural programs to health facilities and education projects—across more than 60 countries.
